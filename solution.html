Novel Cobalt(III) Complexes Incorporating Amino Acid-Reduced Schiff Bases

Solution Design

Solution Design

Hence, our solution to the problem is to successfully synthesise Co3+ complexes incorporating reduced Schiff base ligands in order to characterise and study these complexes and their properties

Starting Materials

Synthesis of [Co(NH3)4CO3]NO3 (SM1)

A solution containing 7.496 g of [Co(OH2)6](NO3)2 dissolved in 15.0 cm3 of H2O was added to a solution, under constant stirring, containing 9.984 g of (NH4)2CO3 dissolved in 30.0 cm3 of H2O and 40.0 cm3 of 25% aqueous NH3. 20.0 cm3 of 6% H2O2 solution was added dropwise to the solution. The solution was concentrated to around 80 cm3 using temperatures no greater than 80.0 ℃ with a total of 2.50 g of (NH4)2CO3 being added in small portions to the solution at 15 min intervals. The solution was left overnight in a refrigerator to crystallise and then suction filtered. Dark purple crystals were obtained after washing the residue with cold H2O and ethanol. The crystals were dried in an oven set at 70.0 ℃.

Synthesis of Na3[Co(CO3)3] (SM2)

A solution containing 2.910 g of Co(OH2)6](NO3)2 dissolved in 5.0 cm3 of H2O and 5.0 cm3 of 6% H2O2 solution was added dropwise to a slurry, under constant stirring, containing 4.200 g of NaHCO3 in 10.0 cm3 of H2O in an ice water bath. After 30 min, the dark green suspension was suction filtered. Dark green solids were obtained after washing the residue with H2O and ethanol. The solids were dried in an oven set at 70.0 ℃.

Synthesis of N-(2-hydroxybenzyl)-amino acids (OH-RSB)

0.751 g of glycine, 0.891 g of alanine or 1.172 g of valine was added to a solution, under constant stirring, containing 0.562 g of KOH dissolved in 20.0 cm3 of 99.9% ethanol and 20.0 cm3 of H2O. 1.062 cm3 of 2-hydroxybenzaldehyde was then added to the solution. After 1 h, 0.454 g of NaBH4 was added to the solution in an ice water bath. After 30 min, glacial acetic acid was added until the pH of the solution was about 6. The solution was left in a refrigerator for 3 days to crystallise and then suction filtered. White crystals were obtained after washing the residue with cold ethanol. The crystals were dried in an oven set at 70.0 ℃.

Synthesis of N-(2-pyridylmethyl)-amino acids (Py-RSB)

0.751 g of glycine, 0.891 g of alanine or 1.172 g of valine was added to a solution, under constant stirring, containing 0.562 g of KOH dissolved in 20.0 cm3 of 99.9% ethanol and 20.0 cm3 of H2O. 0.952 cm3 of 2-pyridinecarboxaldehyde was then added to the solution. After 1 h, 0.454 g of NaBH4 was added to the solution in an ice water bath. After 30 min, glacial acetic acid was added until the pH of the solution was about 6. The solution was then evaporated to dryness and the solid was extracted into hot 99.9% ethanol. The solution was left in a refrigerator for 3 days to crystallise and then suction filtered. White crystals were obtained after washing the residue with cold ethanol. The crystals were dried in an oven set at 70.0 ℃.

Reaction Schemes

Synthesising SM1

2 Co(NO3)2 (aq) + 6 NH3 (aq) + 2 (NH2CO3 (aq) + H2O2 (aq) → 2 [Co(NH3)4CO3]NO3 (aq) + 2 NH4NO3 (aq) + 2H2O (l)


Synthesising SM2

2 Co(NO3)2 (aq) + 6 NaHCO3 (aq) + H2O2 (aq) → 2Na3[Co(CO3)3] (s) + 4 HNO3 (aq) + 2 H2O (l)

Synthesising Schiff bases

Synthesising reduced Schiff bases OH-RSB and Py-RSB

Solvents

At first, we used a variety of solvents ranging from Dimethyl Sulfuroxide, Ethanol, and Acetone. However, all these solvents led to the reactions ran becoming oily and resulted in failures.

Hence, the decision to avoid those solvents was made and we stuck to water as a solvent

Reactions

Reaction 1

0.934 g of Glycine OH-RSB, 1.011 g of Alanine OH-RSB or 1.166 g of Valine OH-RSB was dissolved in 50.0 cm3 of H2O. 0.500g of SM1 was then added and the solution was stirred for 24 h. Brownish-black solutions were obtained and evaporated to give brown oils and black solids.

Reaction 2

0.934 g of Glycine OH-RSB, 1.011 g of Alanine OH-RSB (2b) or 1.166 g of Valine OH-RSB was dissolved in 50.0 cm3 of H2O. 0.500g of SM2 was then added followed by 4.871 cm3 of 2 mol dm3 nitric acid, and the solution was stirred for 24 h. Black solutions were obtained and evaporated to give brown oils.

Reaction 3

0.994 g of Alanine Py-RSB (3a) or 1.381 g of Valine Py-RSB was dissolved in 20.0 cm3 of H2O. 0.500g of SM1 was then added and the solution was stirred for 24 h. Red solutions were obtained to give red solids or red crystals.

Reaction 4

0.994 g of Alanine Py-RSB (3a) or 1.381 g of Valine Py-RSB was dissolved in 20.0 cm3 of H2O. 0.500g of SM2 was then added and the solution was stirred for 24 h. Red solutions were obtained to give red solids or red crystals.
