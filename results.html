Novel Cobalt(III) Complexes Incorporating Amino Acid-Reduced Schiff Bases

Results and Discussion

Reactions with OH-RSB

The reactions of our Cobalt(III) starting materials with the OH-RSB yielded brown oils and black solids instead of the expected colours of red or orange, despite multiple repeats of the reactions. As the products formed were concluded to have decomposed in such a fast time, it was decided to not pursue these reactions any further since the main goal was to synthesise a stable product.

Reactions with Py-RSB

Reactions done with the Cobalt(III) starting materials and Py-RSB yielded red solutions, hence we allowed it to crystallise and sent the crystals for X-ray crystallography (SM2 and N-(2-pyridylmethyl)-valine) and mass spectroscopy.

X-Ray Crystallography

X-ray crystallography was conducted on red crystals from the reaction of cobalt(III) starting materials and Valine Py-RSB, and the same crystal structure was obtained from both crystals. The results show that the N-(2-pyridylmethyl)-DL-valine behaves as a tridentate ligand and binds to the central cobalt(III) ion in a 2:1 ratio, forming an octahedral shape about the cobalt(III) ion. One water of crystallisation was also present in the structure alongside a nitrate counterion.

Mass Spectrums

The spectrums obtained for both products are similar, with the same highest peak at 473 m/z and second highest peak at 429 m/z, corresponding to the complex produced and a fragment of it respectively.

Conclusion and Future Work

Reactions of cobalt(III) starting materials with Valine Py-RSB are found to be the same complex. Further characterisation using elemental analysis and Fourier Transform infrared spectroscopy will be conducted on them. Attempts will be made to obtain crystals of cobalt(III) starting materials with Alanine Py-RSB, and the same characterisation will be conducted on them. Biomedical properties of all four products will then be tested.
