Novel Cobalt(III) Complexes Incorporating Amino Acid-Reduced Schiff Bases

Introduction

Problem identification

There has been a lack of research on cobalt(III) complexes incorporating reduced Schiff base (RSB) ligands.

Hence, this project aims to address and fill in the gap in information regarding cobalt(III) complexes by contributing research efforts.

Background

Cobalt

Cobalt complexes can undergo selective transitions between +2 and +3 oxidation state and contain certain redox properties unobserved in other transition metal species.

It has also been shown to be compatible with the human body as seen in the case of naturally occuring Cobalamin (Vitamin B-12)

Reduced Schiff Bases

Reduced Schiff bases are molecules with a large range of biological activities, not limited to being antibacterial, antiviral, antifungal, antimalarial, antiproliferative, anti-inflammatory and antipyretic

Past Researches

Copper(II), Manganese(II) and Iron(III) reduced Schiff base complexes have displayed considerable catecholase activity (Lu, Song, Hui & Zhang 2012) (Yang, Vetrichelvan, Yang, Moubaraki, Murray & Vittal 2003)

Vanadium Oxide(II) reduced Schiff base complexes have been shown to be better in standards as compared to commercially available bacteria and fungicides (Mangamamba, Garnorkar & Swarnabala 2014)
